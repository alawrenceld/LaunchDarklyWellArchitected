LaunchDarkly Well-Architected

A framework for engineering organizations that build, ship, and govern systems with LaunchDarkly.

LDWA is the answer to "are we using this right?" — an opinionated set of principles, focus areas, best practices, and review questions that describe what a healthy LaunchDarkly-managed system looks like. It is cloud-agnostic, applies whether you run on AWS, GCP, Azure, on-prem, hybrid, or edge, and spans the full LaunchDarkly portfolio: flags, contexts, Guarded Releases, Release Pipelines, AI Configs, Observability, Guardian, and Federal.

The seven pillars

01
Safe Release & Progressive Delivery
Reversible, measurable, progressive exposure for every change.
02
Operational Excellence
Treat the LD layer like the production system it is.
03
Security & Compliance
Govern access and change. Guardian + Federal at home here.
04
Reliability & Resilience
Graceful degradation when SDK, network, or LD itself is impaired.
05
Governance & Artifact Lifecycle
Every artifact has an owner and a retirement plan.
06
Experimentation & Measurement
Hypotheses, metrics, holdouts, captured decisions.
07
Performance & Cost Efficiency
Latency budgets, event volume, MAU/MCI, AI cost guardrails.
+
Lenses
Regulated Industries, AI/GenAI, DX, Mobile, Hybrid, Edge, SaaS, Migration, Growth.

Who it's for

Platform engineers, SREs, release managers, product engineers, EMs and architects, CTOs, solutions architects, and partners — across any LaunchDarkly customer or prospect, regardless of cloud or runtime.

How to use it

Read the framework intro, then pick a pillar that maps to your current pain. Or run a structured workload review using the workbook to surface risks and produce a prioritized improvement plan. Re-review quarterly.
